# Cybercrime Case Study – UPI Payment Fraud

With the rise of digital payments, UPI fraud has become one of the most common cybercrimes among students and young adults.

A common scenario involves a person receiving a call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official or a buyer/seller from an online platform. The scammer asks the victim to approve a “request” on a UPI app, claiming it is needed to receive money. However, instead of receiving money, the victim ends up sending it.

In many cases, scammers also send fake payment links or QR codes, which, when scanned, deduct money from the victim’s account. These frauds usually target people who are not fully aware of how UPI systems work or those who act quickly without verifying the details.

The consequences can be serious, including financial loss and stress. Since transactions happen instantly, it becomes difficult to recover the money once it is sent.

This type of fraud highlights the importance of being cautious while using digital payment platforms and always verifying before taking any action.
